Dataset Details

One hundred clean utterances were randomly selected from the IEEE Sentences dataset, including both male and female speakers. Each utterance has a duration of approximately 3–4 s. A further 100 utterances were drawn from the LibriSpeech corpus, comprising 50 utterances from the test-clean and 50 from the test-other partition, with a mean duration of approximately 10 s. LibriSpeech consists of read speech and is used here primarily because its utterances are longer than those in the IEEE corpus, providing input durations more typical for downstream speech recognition.

Pilot MUSHRA Listening Test

The audio examples in the room tabs (Office 2, Lecture Room 1, Lecture Room 2) are exactly the six samples used in our pilot MUSHRA listening test, drawn from the LibriSpeech sub-100 set described in "Dataset Details". The test followed the MUSHRA paradigm (ITU-R BS.1534) with 11 participants (mix of native and non-native English speakers, ages 20–60). Each participant rated six trials (two utterances × three rooms) covering six conditions: hidden reference, reverberant, WPE, BUDDy, DILmatched, DILCR1. The results below are reported as indications rather than conclusive findings.

Cross-Room (CR1) RIR Configurations

DILCR1 denotes the practical setting in which the candidate room impulse response (RIR) used as the prior is drawn from a different ACE room than the one where the reverberant signal was recorded. We adopt an adjacent-T60 pairing: for each measurement room, the prior is taken from the ACE room whose T60 is closest to that of the measurement room. This represents a moderate, plausible mismatch rather than a worst-case one.

For each measurement room, the same prior is used across all utterances of that room. The RIR ID within the donor room is the one indexed by the H1/H2 selection logic described in the paper.
